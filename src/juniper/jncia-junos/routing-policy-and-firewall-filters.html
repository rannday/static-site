This objective mixes route control and packet filtering. The main exam goal is to understand the structure: terms, matches, actions, and where policies or filters take effect.

What to know

  • Routing policy controls which routes are accepted into a table or advertised out of it.
  • Import policy affects routes being brought into a routing table. Export policy affects routes being advertised by a protocol.
  • By default, Junos routing protocols use built-in behavior until you apply explicit policy.
  • Policies are built from terms. Terms contain match criteria and actions.
  • Firewall filters are stateless packet filters. They match packet fields and then take actions such as accept, discard, reject, count, or classify.
  • Filter terms are evaluated in order, top to bottom, until a match takes effect.
  • Filters can protect transit traffic or traffic destined for the Routing Engine, especially when applied to `lo0`.
  • Unicast RPF is an anti-spoofing check that verifies whether the source is reachable through the expected return path.

Quick checks

  • Policy changes routes. Firewall filters change packet handling.
  • Import = into table. Export = out to neighbors.
  • Order matters for both policy terms and filter terms.
